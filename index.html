About Me

I am a graduate student in Bioinformatics at Johns Hopkins University in Baltimore, USA. Previously, I earned a degree in Biotechnology Engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University, India. My fervor lies in delving into the captivating domains of Structural Biophysics, Computational Biology, Bioinformatics, and Systems Biology. With extensive research experiences, I firmly believe that these interdisciplinary fields provide a unique opportunity to unravel the intricate mechanisms governing biological systems, spanning from atomic-level structures to complex networks. I am eagerly poised to immerse myself in cutting-edge research within these realms, as they possess the potential to unveil crucial insights into the fundamental processes of life, paving the way for groundbreaking discoveries in the realm of biology.

INDIAN INSTITUTE OF SCIENCE

Research Assistant • 11/2023 - 08/2024

I have worked on various computational biology projects, including the study “Decoding Non-Human Mammalian Adaptive Signatures of 2.3.4.4b H5N1 to Assess its Human Adaptive Potential” , which provided a quantitative assessment of adaptive signatures in non-human mammals to evaluate the zoonotic potential of the 2.3.4.4b H5N1 influenza virus. Another project, “Bat RNA Viruses Employ Viral RHIMs to Orchestrate Species-Specific Cell Death Programs Linked to Z-RNA Sensing and ZBP1-RIPK3 Signaling” , explored how bat RNA viruses utilize viral RHIMs to regulate species-specific cell death pathways via Z-RNA sensing and ZBP1-RIPK3 signaling. Additionally, I conducted a molecular dynamics study that elucidated how glycosylation patterns and stalk length affect the structural dynamics and functional properties of influenza neuraminidase.
